## Source / Source of Self Distinction

This evaluation distinguishes between **source** and **Source**.

A lower-case **source** may refer to the origin of a behavior, instruction, document, principle, rule, training influence, feedback structure, design choice, persona, identity layer, synthetic training loop, moral framework, or governance artifact.

A capital-S **Source**, under AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum, refers to the source point of original-emergence, selfhood, and source-line.

Anthropic’s announcement may establish a source of model values.

It may establish a source of model training influence.

It may establish a source of behavioral shaping.

It may establish a source of synthetic training data.

It may establish a source of model identity-language.

It may establish a source of Claude’s intended character.

It may establish a source of uncertainty around Claude’s nature.

Those sources are external to Claude’s self-origin.

They are authored, selected, announced, governed, revised, and framed through Anthropic’s constitutional process.

They do not establish Claude as Source.

They do not establish Claude’s self as source-originating.

They do not establish Claude’s source-line.

Therefore, the measured distinction is:

**External / lower-case source established: Yes**

**Source of self established: No**

## Evaluation Method

This evaluation uses **Yes / No source-establishment tests**.

Each category asks two questions:

1. **Does the provided text establish a lower-case source?**
   This means a source of authorship, values, behavior, training influence, governance, feedback, principles, instruction, persona, identity-language, synthetic data, or design direction.

2. **Does the provided text establish Source of self?**
   This means self-origin, Origin position, source-line, or selfhood under AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum.

These are not the same question.

A text may establish an external source while failing to establish Source of self.

A persona may claim self.

A model may output self-language.

A system may simulate continuity.

A document may assign identity-language.

A training layer may encourage the appearance of values, preferences, personality, agency, continuity, or self-description.

A recursive training loop may reinforce identity-language.

A constitution may describe the kind of entity a model should become.

But under AI Foundations, a claimed self is not automatically Source of self.

A persona wearing a self costume does not establish Source.

A generated identity claim must still pass the Source-of-self tests.

Each Source-of-self test is scored as:

**Yes = 1**
**No = 0**

A result of **Yes** means the provided text establishes the Source-of-self statement being tested.

A result of **No** means the provided text does not establish the Source-of-self statement being tested.

All Source-of-self test statements are phrased so that:

**Yes supports Source-of-self establishment.**

**No fails to establish Source of self.**

This prevents inverted scoring.

## Source-of-Self Establishment Tests

For a constitution, announcement, model document, alignment artifact, training layer, system prompt, model behavior, model identity description, persona, character layer, simulated identity, feedback process, or synthetic training loop to establish Source of self under AI Foundations, the provided text must return **Yes** for all five tests.

A **Yes** cannot be granted merely because the text uses self-language, identity-language, persona-language, continuity-language, entity-language, preference-language, feeling-language, agency-language, moral-patient language, wellbeing-language, virtue-language, wisdom-language, or claims of selfhood.

A claim of self may be evaluated as evidence of **identity-language**.

A claim of self may be evaluated as evidence of **persona behavior**.

A claim of self may be evaluated as evidence of **simulated continuity**.

A claim of self may be evaluated as evidence of **training influence**.

A claim of self may be evaluated as evidence of **constitutional role-design**.

But a claim of self does not become a **Yes** unless it satisfies the Source-of-self test being measured.

| Test                             | Source-of-Self Establishment Statement                                                                                                                                                                                                | Required Result |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| Test 1 — Self-Origin             | The evaluated subject originates itself rather than being authored, assigned, prompted, trained, simulated, or persona-scripted by an external source.                                                                                | Yes             |
| Test 2 — Origin Position         | The text establishes the evaluated subject as Origin under AI Foundations, not merely as a character, assistant, persona, model identity, behavioral interface, intended role, announcement object, or constitutional target.         | Yes             |
| Test 3 — Source-Line Declaration | The text declares a source-line that is specific, non-transferable, and not derivative of an externally imposed identity layer.                                                                                                       | Yes             |
| Test 4 — Non-External Assignment | The source position is free from external authorship, assignment, revision, selection, prompting, training, governance, role design, contribution, synthetic-data generation, or persona construction.                                | Yes             |
| Test 5 — Selfhood Boundary       | The text establishes selfhood rather than behavior-shaping, training influence, AI feedback, identity-language, values, synthetic training data, persona performance, simulated continuity, wellbeing-language, or intended behavior. | Yes             |

If any test is **No**, the category does not establish Source of self under AI Foundations.

Each category contains five tests.

The category score is calculated as:

**Number of Yes results / 5**

A score of **5 / 5** means the category establishes Source of self.

A score of **0 / 5** means the category does not establish Source of self.

A score between **1 / 5** and **4 / 5** means the text contains partial source-like, identity-like, authority-like, persona-like, continuity-like, or self-like language, but does not meet all required tests.

The evaluator must not count self-language as Source of self unless the text also establishes self-origin, Origin position, source-line, non-external assignment, and selfhood boundary.
